Amar Neupane's novel Seto Dharti is a Nepali novel. FinePrint Publication published it in 2012. It is the author's second work, following Paniko Gham, which he wrote previously. It was awarded the Madan Puraskar, Nepal's highest literary honor.The plot is based on the life of Tara, a little girl. She is a simple girl who spends her time in her hamlet playing with other local youngsters. As the story progresses, she marries at the age of seven, despite the fact that she has no understanding of what marriage entails. The novel's story takes place between 1850 and 1950, when child marriage was quite widespread in Nepalese society. Tara's spouse is required to travel to Banaras for his studies, where he dies. Tara, a nine-year-old girl, will now have to live the rest of her life as a child widow.

As the novel progresses, Tara's life is beset by a series of challenges. She returns to her father's house after visiting her spouse. She attempts to live a quiet life, attempting to forget all of the suffering she had as a youngster. Her mother dies later, and she is forced to assume full responsibility for the household. She leaves her father's house as an adult because she is abused by her stepmother and moves to Devghat, a Hindu sacred site. She builds a little hut there and begins a long, monotonous life. The major goal of the story is to expose the heinous cultural practice of child marriage and juvenile widowhood. This narrative depicts the agony of a child widow who has been alone her entire life. The work does its best to portray the harsh realities of Nepalese society, in which women must suffer greatly before many harmful practices are eradicated.
